Grilled Cheese

Description

A grilled cheese is a simple yet delicious sandwich made by grilling slices of bread with melted cheese between them. Typically, butter is spread on the outer sides of the bread before grilling, giving the sandwich a golden, crispy exterior. The cheese inside, often American, cheddar, or a mix of cheeses, becomes gooey and soft as it heats. Itâ€™s often enjoyed as a quick meal or snack, and can be served plain or paired with additions like tomato soup, ham, or other ingredients for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • Bread
  • Cheese
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Butter the bread: Spread butter (or mayonnaise) on one side of each slice of bread.
  2. Assemble the sandwich: Place a slice of cheese between the unbuttered sides of the bread slices, leaving the buttered sides facing outward.
  3. Heat the skillet: Preheat a skillet or frying pan over medium heat.
  4. Grill the sandwich: Place the sandwich in the skillet, buttered side down, and cook for 3-4 minutes until the bread is golden brown.
  5. Flip and cook: Carefully flip the sandwich and grill the other side for another 3-4 minutes, until the cheese is melted and the bread is golden.
  6. Serve: Remove from the pan, slice, and enjoy!
