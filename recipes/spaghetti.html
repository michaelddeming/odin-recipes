Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti is a long, thin, cylindrical type of pasta made from durum wheat semolina, known for its slightly firm texture when cooked to “al dente.” Originating from Italy, spaghetti is a versatile dish that is often served with a wide range of sauces, the most common being marinara or Bolognese, made from tomatoes, garlic, herbs, and meat. It’s frequently paired with parmesan or other cheeses and can also be accompanied by meatballs or vegetables. Spaghetti is boiled in salted water and can be enjoyed as a simple, everyday meal or as part of more elaborate Italian dishes.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) of dried spaghetti
  • Water (for boiling)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for the boiling water)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano or basil (or both)
  • Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. Boil the Spaghetti:
    • Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil.
    • Add 1 tablespoon of salt to the water, then add the spaghetti.
    • Cook according to the package instructions (usually 8-10 minutes) until the spaghetti is “al dente.”
    • Drain and set aside, reserving about 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  2. Make the Marinara Sauce:
    • Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    • Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened.
    • Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds to 1 minute, until fragrant.
    • Pour in the crushed tomatoes, stirring well.
    • Add the dried oregano or basil, sugar (if using), and season with salt and pepper to taste.
    • Let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. You can add some reserved pasta water if the sauce becomes too thick.
  3. Combine Spaghetti and Sauce:
    • Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss to combine, making sure the pasta is evenly coated.
  4. Serve:
    • Serve the spaghetti hot, topped with fresh basil (optional) and grated Parmesan cheese.
